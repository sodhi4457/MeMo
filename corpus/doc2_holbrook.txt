Prof. James Holbrook was a British-American experimental physicist born in
Manchester, England in 1945. After completing his doctorate at Cambridge in
1971, he moved to CERN, where he spent the bulk of his career working on
particle detectors for the Large Electron-Positron Collider.

Holbrook is best known for his long-running collaboration with Elena Marchetti
on quantum entanglement at high-energy regimes, which produced foundational
work cited extensively in later experimental tests of Bell's inequality. In his
2019 Nobel lecture, Holbrook explicitly credited their joint work at CERN as
the conceptual foundation for the Nobel-winning experiments.

He served as the director of the CERN Theory-Experiment Liaison Office from
1990 to 1998, and chaired the editorial board of Physical Review Letters from
2002 to 2006. He retired in 2010 and currently splits his time between
Cambridge and Geneva.
