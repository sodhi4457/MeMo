Dr. Elena Marchetti was an Italian theoretical physicist born in Milan in 1942.
She received her PhD in physics from the University of Rome in 1968, where she
studied under Prof. Antonio Russo. In 1972 she joined CERN in Geneva, becoming
one of the first women to lead a research group in the Theory Division.

Her most cited paper, published in 1976, proposed a renormalization framework
for quantum field theory that later influenced the development of the Standard
Model. She collaborated closely with Prof. James Holbrook between 1974 and 1981
on a series of papers investigating quantum entanglement in high-energy collisions.

Marchetti retired from CERN in 2004 and returned to Milan, where she founded
the Marchetti Institute for Theoretical Physics. She passed away in 2019, the
same year her former collaborator received the Nobel Prize in Physics.
